## Purpose

This document explains how the education-first telemedicine strategy
is adapted for the United States and Canada healthcare markets.

---

## United States Market Considerations

### Regulatory Environment
- HIPAA compliance
- FTC truth-in-advertising standards
- Strict platform enforcement on health claims

### Strategy Adaptations
- Strong emphasis on disclaimers
- No outcome-based or diagnostic claims
- Doctor-led education instead of promotional ads
- Heavy use of content, webinars, and SEO

### Channels That Perform Well
- LinkedIn (B2B healthcare & HR)
- Google Search (educational intent)
- Email (first-party consent only)

---

## Canada Market Considerations

### Regulatory Environment
- PHIPA & PIPEDA data privacy standards
- Health Canada advertising restrictions

### Strategy Adaptations
- Preventive and wellness-focused messaging
- Strong transparency around data use
- Public-health-aligned education tone

### Channels That Perform Well
- Educational blogs
- Webinars
- Employer-focused healthcare content

---

## Why Education-First Works in North America

- Regulators favor public health education
- Patients are skeptical of aggressive health ads
- Trust drives long-term LTV
- Reduces CAC and ad account risk
